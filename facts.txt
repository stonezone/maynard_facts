# Maynard James Keenan Facts
Cat Fact: Maynard James Keenan (born James Herbert Keenan on April 17, 1964 in Ravenna, Ohio) isn't just the voice of Tool—he's also a winemaker, record producer and philanthropist.
Toolism: Before forming Tool, Maynard served as a forward observer in the U.S. Army, joining after high school to use the G.I. Bill for art school.
Meow-sical Journey: While studying at Kendall College of Art and Design and working as an interior designer in Los Angeles, he began writing an early version of Tool's first hit "Sober."
Backstage Cat: On stage, Maynard likes to stand on a platform at the back facing away from the crowd so he can retreat into his own headspace and take cues from his bandmates.
Fibonacci Feline: The syllable pattern in Tool's song "Lateralus" follows the Fibonacci sequence—because Maynard loves mixing math with melody.
Purrfect Range: Maynard's baritone voice reportedly spans four octaves, giving him a vocal flexibility as wide as a lion's roar.
Puscifer Kitten: In the 1990s, Maynard popped up on HBO's Mr. Show as the frontman of a fake band called Puscifer—years before Puscifer became a real project.
April Fools Furball: Tool once pranked fans on April 1st by announcing that Maynard had "found Jesus" and was leaving the band; a week later he laughed it off.
Wine & Whiskers: Inspired by his Italian relatives, Maynard runs Merkin Vineyards and Caduceus Cellars in Arizona and even opened an organic market with a tasting room.
Vineyard Tribute: He scattered his mother Judith's ashes in one of his vineyards and named a wine "Nagual del Judith" after her—honoring her memory with grapes.
Community Cat: Maynard has performed at benefit shows for RAINN and tsunami relief, and lends his voice to Axis of Justice, a musicians' social-justice coalition.
Sneaky Cat: He's so private that he once carried business cards reading "Jesus H. Christ" and keeps trespassers away with a paintball gun.
Black Belt Kitty: A longtime Brazilian jiu-jitsu practitioner, Maynard earned his black belt on January 6, 2024—celebrated by posing his new belt next to a bottle of his own wine.
Stage Takedown: During a concert, a fan jumped on stage and Maynard calmly applied a jiu-jitsu move to subdue him—because self-control matters.
Family Chorus: Maynard's son Devo sang backing vocals on A Perfect Circle's Thirteenth Step and played cello at his dad's 50th-birthday show.
Free Frances Beans: In the mid-90s, Maynard created "Free Frances Bean" T-shirts as a tongue-in-cheek commentary, and they became an underground hit.
Acting Paws: He made cameo appearances in the film Crank: High Voltage and on Comedy Bang! Bang! as a fictional punk rocker.
Autobiography Yarn: His memoir, A Perfect Union of Contrary Things, released in 2016, chronicles his life before and beyond Tool.
Philosofur: Maynard's lyrics explore philosophy, Jungian psychology and evolution—encouraging listeners to look inward rather than just sing along.
Desert Vintage: Early wines from his vineyards were named after relatives of Apache chief Cochise before he released Arizona's first 100% estate wine in 2009.
Army of One: Inspired by Bill Murray's movie Stripes, he enlisted in the Army and studied at West Point Prep School, where he wrestled, ran cross-country and sang in the glee club.
Grape Escape: Maynard's passion for wine stems from his grandparents and great-uncle in Italy; his vineyards have won awards and he loves blending grapes like chords.
Collaborative Cat: He has collaborated with artists such as Rage Against the Machine, Trent Reznor and Deftones, showing his willingness to cross genres.
Private Paw: Despite fame, he resides in rural Arizona, rarely gives interviews and emerges only for causes and the occasional comedic sketch.
Fiery Feline: Tool's 1996 album Aenima sold millions and won a Grammy for Best Metal Performance in 1998, cementing Maynard's place as progressive metal royalty.

# Tool Band History & Formation
Tool Origin: Tool was formed in Los Angeles in 1990 when vocalist Maynard James Keenan and guitarist Adam Jones were introduced by their girlfriends, who noticed their similar taste in music and art.
Reluctant Drummer: Danny Carey initially refused invitations to join Tool, but showed up when the band's original drummer didn't appear for a session—after one jam, he was in.
Original Bassist: Tool's founding bassist Paul D'Amour played on the band's first two releases before leaving in 1995, making way for Justin Chancellor.
Demo Days: Tool's first recording was a 1991 demo EP called "72826"—the band signed to Zoo Entertainment just three months into their career.
Opiate Debut: Tool released their first studio EP, Opiate, in March 1992, launching their career with the aggressive title track.
Undertow Success: Tool's 1993 debut full-length album Undertow was eventually certified triple platinum by the RIAA.
Bill Hicks Tribute: Tool's 1996 album Aenima was dedicated to stand-up comedian Bill Hicks, who died in 1994. The band felt they were "resonating similar concepts."
Lateralus Math: The title track "Lateralus" incorporates the Fibonacci sequence into both its lyrics and drum patterns—a mathematical spiral in musical form.
Number One Debut: Tool's 2001 album Lateralus reached number one on the US Billboard 200 in its debut week.
10,000 Days Sales: Tool's 2006 album 10,000 Days sold 550,000 copies in its first week in the United States alone.
Streaming Holdout: Tool was one of the last major rock bands to release their music on streaming platforms, finally doing so on August 2, 2019.

# Fear Inoculum (2019)
13-Year Wait: Fear Inoculum was Tool's first album in 13 years, following 2006's 10,000 Days—the delay was caused by creative struggles, side projects, and legal issues.
Longest Hot 100 Song: At 10 minutes and 22 seconds, the title track "Fear Inoculum" became the longest song in Billboard Hot 100 history, breaking David Bowie's record.
Third Number One: Fear Inoculum debuted at Number One in America, becoming Tool's third US chart-topping album.
Wine Harvest Recording: Maynard was so busy tending to his wine grapes that producers traveled to his Arizona home during harvest season to record his vocals.
One-Take Wonder: Danny Carey nailed his fast, complicated drum part on "Chocolate Chip Trip" in a single take while the rest of the band was out at lunch.
Studio Fire: During recording, one of the candles Danny Carey lit to set the mood in the studio nearly caught fire.
Seven Theme: While working on songs, Adam Jones noticed many riffs were in sevens—Maynard revealed he had a lyrical concept based on the number seven, and artist Alex Grey had visual ideas for the theme too.
Dumped and Restarted: Justin Chancellor described dumping entire albums worth of material and starting over multiple times during the 13-year creative process.
20 Song Ideas: At one point during Fear Inoculum's development, guitarist Adam Jones reported that the band had 20 different song ideas being worked on simultaneously.

# Grammy Awards
Four-Time Winners: Tool has won four Grammy Awards throughout their career.
First Grammy: Tool won their first Grammy for Best Metal Performance in 1998 for the song "Aenema."
Schism Victory: The single "Schism" from Lateralus won a Grammy Award for Best Metal Performance in 2002.
Satan Thanks: During Tool's Grammy acceptance speech for "Schism," drummer Danny Carey thanked his parents "for putting up with him" and Satan.
Package Deal: 10,000 Days won Best Recording Package at the 49th Grammy Awards for Adam Jones' 3-D stereoscopic artwork.
7empest Triumph: On January 26, 2020, Tool won Best Metal Performance for "7empest" at the 62nd Grammy Awards.

# Tool in Hawaii
Aloha Return: Tool announced a concert at Honolulu's Blaisdell Arena on December 19, 2025—their first show in Hawaii since 2011.
14-Year Gap: The 2025 Honolulu show marks Tool's return to Hawaii after nearly 15 years, making it a rare opportunity for island fans.
Same Venue: Tool will return to Blaisdell Arena, the same venue where they last performed on January 14, 2011 during the 10,000 Days tour.
Pacific Tour: The Honolulu stop extends Tool's December 2025 international run, which also includes shows in Yokohama and Kobe, Japan.

# A Perfect Circle
Supergroup Formation: A Perfect Circle was formed in 1999 by guitarist Billy Howerdel and Tool vocalist Maynard James Keenan in Los Angeles.
Roommate Demo: Billy Howerdel was a guitar tech for Nine Inch Nails and Tool when Maynard offered him a room in his North Hollywood home in 1995—there, Howerdel played demos that convinced Maynard to sing.
Record-Breaking Debut: A Perfect Circle's debut album Mer de Noms (2000) entered the Billboard 200 at No. 4—the highest ever debut for a rock band's first album at the time.
Sea of Names: "Mer de Noms" is French for "Sea of Names"—most tracks are named after people Maynard knew, including "Judith" (his mother), "Orestes," and "Thomas."
Thirteenth Step Theme: A Perfect Circle's second album Thirteenth Step (2003) centers on themes of addiction and recovery, referencing 12-step programs with the title suggesting ongoing maintenance of sobriety.
Higher Debut: Thirteenth Step debuted even higher than Mer de Noms, charting at number 2 and selling over 233,000 copies in its opening week.
Lost Track Found: The song "Vanishing" was supposed to be on Mer de Noms, but Howerdel lost the file because he'd named it "Test"—he found it years later and it appeared on Thirteenth Step.
Eat the Elephant: After a 14-year hiatus, A Perfect Circle released Eat the Elephant in 2018, their fourth studio album.
Sessanta Tour: In 2024, A Perfect Circle joined Puscifer and Primus for the Sessanta tour celebrating Maynard's 60th birthday.

# Puscifer
Fictional to Real: The name "Puscifer" first appeared as a fictional band on HBO's Mr. Show in the 1990s—years later, Maynard made it real.
Creative Playground: Maynard describes Puscifer as "a playground for the various voices in my head—a space where my Id, Ego, and Anima all come together to exchange cookie recipes."
Tour Bus Album: Puscifer's debut album "V" Is for Vagina (2007) was created on tour buses, in hotel rooms, and various studios while Maynard toured with Tool.
Independent Success: "V" Is for Vagina debuted at number 1 on the Billboard Independent Albums chart.
All-Star Guests: Early Puscifer recordings featured Tim Alexander (Primus), Tim Commerford and Brad Wilk (Rage Against the Machine), and actress Milla Jovovich.
Jerome Store: The first Puscifer store opened on October 3, 2008, in Jerome, Arizona—the same town where Maynard makes wine—above a tattoo parlor.
Fashion Brand: Puscifer is "as much a clothing line as it is a band," selling merchandise from their Arizona storefront.
Bohemian Cover: In 2013, Puscifer released an EP featuring covers of Queen's "Bohemian Rhapsody" and Accept's "Balls to the Wall."

# Danny Carey (Drummer)
Towering Talent: Danny Carey stands at 6'5"—one of the tallest drummers in rock music.
Rolling Stone Ranked: Danny Carey was ranked 26th among the 100 greatest drummers of all time by Rolling Stone magazine.
Feet Like Hands: Carey practices rudiments and snare drum solos with his feet to improve his double bass drumming and foot independence.
Masonic Inspiration: As a young child, Danny watched his father conduct a Masonic ritual—the gestures fascinated him and later influenced his interest in sacred geometry.
Occult Scholar: Danny Carey has studied the occult extensively, citing 16th-century English mathematician and astrologer John Dee as a favorite influence.
Sacred Geometry Kit: Danny's drum kit is often arranged according to sacred geometry principles, and he's developed polyrhythmic patterns derived from geometric figures like the unicursal hexagram.
Tabla Training: Carey studied tabla with master Aloke Dutta and plays tabla himself on Tool tracks like "Disposition" and "Right in Two."
King Crimson Connection: In 2024, Danny Carey toured with the supergroup BEAT, playing 1980s King Crimson music alongside Adrian Belew, Tony Levin, and Steve Vai.
Dual Time Signatures: In "Lateralus," the band plays in 6/8 while Danny simultaneously plays in 5/8—creating complex polyrhythmic textures.

# Adam Jones (Guitarist)
Hollywood FX: Before Tool, Adam Jones worked in special effects on blockbuster films including Jurassic Park, Terminator 2, Batman Returns, Ghostbusters II, and Predator 2.
Nightmare Creator: Adam Jones created the "Freddy Krueger in the womb" makeup for A Nightmare on Elm Street 5: The Dream Child.
Self-Taught: Despite his legendary guitar skills, Adam Jones never took formal guitar lessons—he learned entirely by ear.
Video Director: Adam Jones directs most of Tool's music videos, applying stop-motion techniques he learned in the film industry.
Tom Morello Connection: Before moving to California, Adam Jones played bass in a band called Electric Sheep with future Rage Against the Machine guitarist Tom Morello.
Rolling Stone Guitarist: Adam Jones was rated the 75th-greatest guitarist of all time by Rolling Stone and placed 9th in Guitar World's Top 100 Greatest Metal Guitarists.
3-D Packaging: Jones designed 10,000 Days' packaging with stereoscopic lenses for viewing 3-D artwork, winning a Grammy for Best Recording Package.

# Justin Chancellor (Bassist)
British Import: Justin Chancellor is English, born November 19, 1971—he moved from London to join Tool in 1995.
Peach Origins: Before Tool, Justin co-founded the alternative metal band Peach in London in 1991, releasing the album "Giving Birth to a Stone."
Tool Connection: Peach supported Tool on their 1994 European tour, and when bassist Paul D'Amour left, Tool called Justin—though he initially turned them down.
Signature Wal Bass: Justin's main instrument is a Wal MKII 4-string bass he bought on a friend's recommendation during the recording of Aenima.
Death Grips Collab: In 2018, Justin Chancellor collaborated with experimental hip-hop group Death Grips on the track "Disappointed."
Primus Narration: Justin provided narration on the Primus track "The Valley" from their 2017 album The Desaturating Seven.
Topanga Store: Justin and his wife once ran a store called Lobal Orning in Topanga, California, dedicated to music and literature "that shaped and changed" them.

# Philosophical & Artistic Influences
Literary Inspirations: Tool draws inspiration from authors and philosophers including Joseph Campbell, Carl Jung, Aleister Crowley, William Gibson, and Ronald P. Vincent.
Alex Grey Art: Tool has collaborated extensively with visionary artist Alex Grey, whose transcendent imagery appears on Lateralus, 10,000 Days, and Fear Inoculum artwork.
Think for Yourself: The quote "Think for yourself. Question authority" appears in Tool's music—originally from Timothy Leary, it became a band motto.
